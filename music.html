Music – Musica

The Spanish Colonial Period greatly influenced Filipino music by introducing new instruments, melodies, and religious songs. Traditions such as the harana, rondalla, and church hymns reflect the blending of Spanish styles with Filipino creativity. These musical influences became part of the country’s cultural identity and continue to be heard in modern Filipino music today.

Experience a heartfelt moment as a man serenades his wife with a song in the romantic surroundings of Intramuros, Manila. This emotional gesture reflects the Filipino tradition of harana, which was influenced by Spanish colonial culture and emphasizes love, music, and courtship. Captured by Brylle Sarong on March 30, 2026 in Fort Santiago, Intramuros this scene beautifully highlights the timeless romance and heritage preserved within the historic walls of Intramuros.

Witness the cultural harmony of Filipino heritage through a man playing the bandurria, a traditional string instrument often used in Spanish-influenced folk music. Introduced during the Spanish colonial period, the bandurria became an essential part of rondalla ensembles in the Philippines, symbolizing both artistry and cultural fusion. Captured by Arles Clutario on April 4, 2026, at Taguig City, this moment reflects the enduring musical legacy shaped by Spanish influence.
