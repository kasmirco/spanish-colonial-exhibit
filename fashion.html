Fashion - Moda

The Spanish Colonial Period greatly influenced Filipino fashion by introducing elegant clothing styles made from fine fabrics and detailed embroidery. Traditional attire such as the baro’t saya and barong Tagalog evolved from Spanish-inspired designs blended with local materials and craftsmanship. Such as the formal version of baro’t saya Maria Clara Gown for women and Camisa de chino for men.

A multi-generational family poses in traditional Filipino attire inspired by the Spanish colonial era. The group is dressed in garments such as the baro’t saya, terno, and barong, with intricate fabrics, lace details, and hand-held fans. The setting features a preserve Spanish colonial houses in Batangas. This photo was captured by Justin Lui Palentinos on March 29, 2026 at Casa Recuerdos De Taal, Batangas.

A traditional Filipiniana dress stands on display at the National Museum of Fine Arts, captured by Juliana Mhay Caballero on April 19, 2026, illustrating the style worn by Filipino women during the Spanish colonial era. The piece highlights the fusion of local craftsmanship and European influence, preserved in the museum to showcase the country’s cultural and historical identity.

On April 19, 2026, Senior High School graduates from Pamantasan ng Lungsod ng Maynila gathered at the National Museum of Fine Arts in Ermita, Manila, for a photo to commemorate their achievements. In the background, they stood before Juan Luna’s Spoliarium in their graduation attire of traditional Baro’t Saya and Barong Tagalog, reflecting the enduring influence of the Spanish regime on the nation's identity. This photo was captured by Samantha Lopez at April 19, 2026 at Manila

Celebrate the vibrant flavors of paella, a world-famous rice dish traditionally prepared with saffron, vegetables, seafood, or meat. Originating from Spain, paella became one of the most iconic dishes that influenced culinary traditions in many countries, including the Philippines. Captured by Ara Go on April 21, 2026 at Taguig City, this flavorful dish reflects the rich heritage of Spanish cuisine.
