Food - Alimentos

The Spanish Colonial Period greatly influenced Filipino cuisine, introducing new ingredients, cooking methods, and dishes that became part of local culture. Popular foods such as adobo, menudo, leche flan, paella and etc reflect the blending of Spanish flavors with Filipino traditions. Through time, these culinary influences became symbols of heritage and remain present in everyday Filipino dining.

Savor the irresistible taste of churros, a classic fried pastry famous for its crunchy texture and sweet cinnamon sugar coating. Originally introduced during the Spanish era, churros have become a popular snack enjoyed in many parts of the world. Captured by Janil Beljot on April 21, 2026 at Pasig City, this delicious delicacy represents the enduring legacy of Spanish culinary traditions.

Experience the soft and buttery delight of ensaymada, a popular Filipino pastry topped with sugar, grated cheese, and sometimes buttercream. Derived from the Spanish ensaimada, this sweet bread became a cherished part of Filipino baking traditions and is often enjoyed during breakfast or merienda. Captured by Jinelle Balading on April 21, 2026 at Pasig City, this pastry reflects the lasting influence of Spanish culture on Filipino cuisine.

Discover the rich flavors of menudo, a well-loved Filipino dish made with tender pork, liver, potatoes, carrots, and tomato-based sauce. Influenced by Spanish cuisine during the colonial period, menudo became a staple in Filipino celebrations and family gatherings. Captured by Janil Beljot on April 21, 2026, at Pasig City, this savory dish reflects the blend of Spanish and Filipino culinary heritage.

Celebrate the vibrant flavors of paella, a world-famous rice dish traditionally prepared with saffron, vegetables, seafood, or meat. Originating from Spain, paella became one of the most iconic dishes that influenced culinary traditions in many countries, including the Philippines. Captured by Ara Go on April 21, 2026 at Taguig City, this flavorful dish reflects the rich heritage of Spanish cuisine.

Experience the satisfying crunch of chicharon, a popular snack made from deep-fried pork rind known for its crispy texture and savory flavor. Introduced through Spanish culinary influence, chicharon became a well-loved delicacy in the Philippines and is often enjoyed as a snack or paired with local dishes. Captured by Hans Mabao on April 28, 2026 at Taguig City this crunchy treat reflects the lasting connection between Spanish and Filipino food traditions.

Enjoy the rich and savory taste of chicken adobo, a beloved Filipino dish made with tender chicken simmered in soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, and spices. While rooted in native Filipino cooking methods, the name adobo was given during the Spanish colonial period, reflecting the blend of local and Spanish influences. Captured by Jannah Tamayo on April 21, 2026 at Taguig City, this iconic dish represents the enduring heritage of Filipino cuisine.

Brazo de Mercedes is a Filipino dessert made from a soft meringue roll filled with rich custard, developed in the Philippines during the Spanish colonial period using European baking techniques and locally adapted ingredients. Captured by Raicelle Valdez on April 21, 2026 at Pasig City, this dessert reflects the lasting Spanish influence on Philippine pastry culture.
